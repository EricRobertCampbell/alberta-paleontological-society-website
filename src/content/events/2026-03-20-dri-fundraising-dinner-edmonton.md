The Dinosaur Research Institute is hosting its **1st Fundraising Dinner in Edmonton** at 6 p.m. on Friday, March 20, 2026 in the Winspear Dining Room at the University Club (11435 Saskatchewan Drive).

**Dr. Philip Currie**, professor of Dinosaur Palaeobiology in the Department of Biological Sciences at the University of Alberta, will give the main presentation: **"The History of the Growth of Dinosaur Palaeontology in Alberta"**.

PhD and Master's students from the University of Alberta will present on their work, and grad students will display posters of their latest research. A student will be seated at each table to answer paleo questions and discuss their research.

**Tickets:** $200.00 per person. A tax receipt will be issued for approximately ½ of the ticket price.

**Silent auction** items include jewellery, wine, event packages, and art.

**Dinner options:** Salmon, chicken, or vegetarian (main course). Please specify your entrée preference and any food allergies or dietary concerns when requesting tickets.

**To request tickets:** Email [info@dinosaurresearch.com](mailto:info@dinosaurresearch.com) or call Roger (780-951-5482, [Roger@nortecplumbing.ca](mailto:Roger@nortecplumbing.ca)) or Al (403-861-0532).

**Payment:** Interac e-Transfer to info@dinosaurresearch.com, cheque to Dinosaur Research Institute, 2835-12 Ave. NW, Calgary T2P 1K9, or credit/debit card.

Roger Lebeuf is leading the organizing committee for the Edmonton Dinner.

For additional information, see [the event flyer (PDF)](/events/2026/external/DRIDinnerInEdmontonMarch20_2026.pdf).

The Dinosaur Research Institute was established in 1996 to support paleo research. In 2025 they provided more than $47,000 to support students in dinosaur research projects and fieldwork.
