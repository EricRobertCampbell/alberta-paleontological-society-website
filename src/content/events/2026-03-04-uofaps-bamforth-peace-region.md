Southern Alberta has long been regarded as Canada's dinosaur hotspot, but northwest Alberta's Peace Region also contains significant dinosaur-aged deposits. The most famous of these is the Pipestone Creek Pachyrhinosaurus Bonebed outside of Grande Prairie, but new fossil sites and bonebeds discovered in the last decade are revealing more about these northern dinosaur ecosystems.

This talk will focus on new discoveries and current work being done by the Philip J. Currie Museum and the U of A's Boreal Alberta Dinosaur Project (BADP), and on how students and volunteers can get involved.
