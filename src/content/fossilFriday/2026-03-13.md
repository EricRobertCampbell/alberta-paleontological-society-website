In honour of how much snow fell over the past few days, this Fossil Friday is a set of fossilized droppings (coprolites)!

These were collected from the Late Cretaceous Whitemud Formation in Saskatchewan by Karen Weinhold. Coprolites are often recognizable by their distinctive shape. You can also sometimes tell them apart from other rocks by their smell - the phosphorus in the original droppings leads to different minerals in the resulting fossil!

This is APS 1990-004, and the scale bar is 1cm.
