This fossil is the feeding appendage of *Anomalocaris canadensis*! When originally discovered it was thought to be the headless body of a shrimp, but was later discovered to be part of one of the Middle Cambrian's largest and fiercest predators. While only getting to about a metre long, this would have made *Anomalocaris* one of the largest living things on Earth when it lived, around 510 Ma.

This fossil was found at the Mt. Stephen trilobite beds in Yoho National Park. This site features spectacular soft-body preservation and an enormous quantity of trilobites. It's in a restricted area, so you'll need to book a tour if you want to see it for yourself!
