This week's Fossil Friday is a tooth of *Sandalodus*, a Carboniferous shark. This animal was swimming in the tropical seas that covered Alberta roughly 350 million years ago. At that time, much of Alberta lay under a warm, shallow sea - more like the Bahamas today than the prairies we know now! Those waters teemed with fish and invertebrates, offering plenty of prey for *Sandalodus*.

This specimen was found near Bragg Creek in southern Alberta. It is APS 1985.020 and was collected by Geoffrey Barrett.
