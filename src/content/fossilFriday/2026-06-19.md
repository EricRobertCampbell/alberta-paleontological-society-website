For this Fossil Friday we have the claw bone (ungual phalanx) from a small theropod from the Dinosaur Park Formation in southern Alberta. It's not clear exactly what species this bone is from, but there are a number of candidates, including *Troodon* and *Saurornitholestes* (a cousin of *Velociraptor*). In life this bone would have been covered in a sheath of keratin, making it much larger!

This specimen was found by Hope Johnson and is APS 2006.071. Photo credit Howard Allen.
