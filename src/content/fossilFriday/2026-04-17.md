This week's Fossil Friday is a complete phacopid trilobite from the Ordovician, still in its shale matrix. Trilobites are extinct arthropods with a distinctive three-segmented exoskeleton: the head (cephalon), body (thorax), and tail (pygidium). Despite this their name actually refers to the three longitudinal lobes: a central axial lobe and the left and right pleural lobes.

This specimen was donated by Dr. Monika Shulz and is catalogued as APS2013.002.
